Code Name Bananas

By David Walliams


As bombs rain down on London, eleven-year-old Eric must rescue Gertrude, a gorilla at the London Zoo with the help of his uncle. But while hiding with Uncle Sid and Gertrude, the three of them uncover a top-secret Nazi plot. This book is very exciting. The combination of story writing and character development makes this book a good read.

To Be a Cat

By Matt Haig


Barney was definitely not the happiest boy in the world. He was bullied by Gavin Needle, a classmate, and even the principal of his school. He wanted nothing more than to be someone other than himself. One day, he makes a wish...a wish he regrets immediately. This book is one of the most interesting books I’ve read in a long time. It opens up your imagination to the impossible and the improbable.

The Night Bus Hero

By Onjali Q. Rauf


People don’t expect much from a prankster like Hector. But when famous statues start disappearing around London, he decides to change people’s opinion of him by catching the thieves. He decides to become a hero. This book made me feel happy at a the thought of a person being able to change people’s perspectives about them. I enjoyed the storytelling.
